## Check adjustment sets

Open a DAG page and inspect the graph with **dagitty.js**-derived metadata. Compare the proposed adjustment set against your own identification strategy before running analyses.

## Make hypotheses explicit

Use published DAGs as templates: fork the structure, document which edges you keep or drop, and cite edge-level evidence. DAGpedia entries are meant to travel across papers and contexts with clear changelogs in git.

## Teach causal inference

Instructors can link to stable URLs instead of redrawing the same textbook DAG each semester. Narrative sections document operationalization choices that slides often omit.

## Reuse and adapt

Under [CC BY 4.0](/about/license), you may adapt diagrams with attribution. Copy the DAGitty block into R or DAGitty desktop tools for further queries.

## Contribute back

When you extend a graph for a new population or exposure definition, consider opening a pull request so the community benefits from your edge rationale and citations. See [How to contribute](/about/contributing).
